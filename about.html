About The Notleys Golf Club

An 18-hole, 6,116-yard par 71 golf course set in the River Brain valley at White Notley.

Book a Tee Time View the Course

Background

Golf in the Brain valley.

The Notleys Golf Club sits on Witham Road in White Notley, between Witham and Braintree. The course runs through the valley of the River Brain, with open views across the surrounding Essex countryside.

It is a golf club first: members, visitors, beginners, and regular players using the course, practice areas, coaching, and clubhouse around day-to-day play.

The club has historically been informal and accessible. The current direction is to keep that openness while making standards, introductions, and guidance clearer.

At a glance

Course and club basics

Course

18 holes, 6,116 yards, par 71, set in the River Brain valley.

Play

A fair challenge for experienced golfers, while still approachable for beginners.

Membership

Membership options for regular golf, club competitions, and gaining an official handicap.

Clubhouse

A traditional Essex barn-style clubhouse with a decked area overlooking the course.

Course setting

Playable, open, and shaped by the valley.

The layout gives wide views across the course and surrounding countryside. It is not a closed-in course, and the valley setting is part of the appeal of playing here regularly.

The River Brain affects several holes. The round also changes beyond the railway line, where flatter holes bring ditches and ponds into play.

The course should not feel out of reach for newer golfers, but regular players still need to think about position, pace, and course management.

Club character

Open to members, visitors, and beginners.

The club is non-elitist by nature. You do not need a long background in golf to play here, and many golfers start from zero at The Notleys.

Visitors are welcome, and membership is there for people who want to play more often, build a handicap, and enter competitions.

Current direction

More structure, not more barriers.

The club has historically been informal. The aim now is to keep the access while making expectations clearer for new and regular players.

That means better introductions, better guidance, respect for the course, pace of play, and basic etiquette explained plainly.

Facilities

Useful before and after the round.

The clubhouse gives players a base before and after a round. The golf facilities support practice, coaching, and getting around the course.

Driving range

Covered bays for warm-ups, practice, and lessons.

Practice areas

Short-game and putting spaces for simple, regular work.

Coaching

Support for beginners and players working on specific parts of their game.

Buggy hire

Available when course conditions and daily status allow.

Clubhouse

A barn-style base for check-in, food, drink, and time after the round.
