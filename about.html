Golf that is easy to start and worth coming back to.

The Notleys is an accessible, non-elitist golf club in the River Brain valley. Eighteen holes, par 71, and a place where you can turn up, learn, play, and keep improving.

A golf club first

A place to play golf, not protect an image.

The Notleys exists for people who want to play. The club is not built around formality, status, or making golf feel harder to enter than it needs to be.

Members and visitors use it as a real club: book a round, get out on the course, practise when needed, and come back regularly.

You do not need a golfing background to start here. Plenty of people first pick up the game at The Notleys and learn the basics without having to pretend they already know how everything works.

The course

Playable, repeatable, still interesting.

The course is an 18-hole parkland layout measuring 6,116 yards, playing to par 71. It sits in the River Brain valley, with natural changes in level and open views across the course.

The River Brain comes into play on several holes. It adds decisions and consequences, but the course is still playable enough to suit regular rounds, practice rounds, and people building confidence.

18 holes Par 71 6,116 yards

Getting started

Starting golf should be clearer.

Golf can be hard to read from the outside. What to book, where to check in, how to use the range, and what is expected on the course are not always obvious when you are new.

The club has historically been informal, which has helped keep barriers low. The next step is better introductions, clearer guidance, and a smoother route in for new golfers.

Playing standards

Welcoming does not mean careless.

Good golf culture is simple: look after the course, keep pace with the group in front, and show basic respect to other players.

The aim is not to make the club stiff or intimidating. It is to make the course better for everyone who plays it, from first rounds to weekly medals.

Golf facilities

Useful, practical, golf-focused.

Facilities stay focused on playing and improving: practice, coaching, getting around the course, and a practical clubhouse base.

Driving range

Covered bays for warm-ups, practice, and lessons.

Practice areas

Short-game and putting spaces for simple, regular work.

Coaching

Support for beginners and players working on specific parts of their game.

Buggy hire

Available when course conditions and daily status allow.

Clubhouse

A base for check-in, food, drink, and time after the round.

The direction

Keep the access. Improve the experience.

The direction is simple: keep the club open, accessible, and non-elitist, while making the everyday playing experience clearer.

That means better onboarding, more consistent expectations, and playing standards that support the people who use the course week after week.
