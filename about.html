About The Notleys Golf Club

An 18-hole parkland course at White Notley, set in the River Brain valley and known today as The Notleys.

Book a Tee Time View the Course

Background

A local course with a few names behind it.

The Notleys Golf Club sits on Witham Road in White Notley, between Witham and Braintree. The course was established in the mid-1990s and has been known under more than one name.

Older course listings refer to Brain Valley Golf Club and later Lords Golf and Country Club, Notley. The current name is simpler: The Notleys Golf Club.

The course itself is the constant: 18 holes in the River Brain valley, used by members, visitors, beginners, and regular golfers.

At a glance

What is known

Opened

Public course listings record the course as opening in 1995.

Earlier names

Brain Valley Golf Club appears in older references. Lords Golf and Country Club, Notley appears later.

Current name

The club now operates as The Notleys Golf Club.

Location

Witham Road, White Notley, on the course land running through the River Brain valley.

Course setting

The River Brain is part of the round.

The course is a parkland layout measuring 6,116 yards from the white tees and playing to par 71.

The River Brain affects several holes. The round also changes beyond the railway line, where flatter holes bring ditches and ponds into play.

It is playable enough for newer golfers, but there is still enough in the layout for regular players to stay interested.

Club character

Open to regular players and beginners.

The Notleys has not built itself around exclusivity. People use it because they can get on the course, learn the game, and play regularly.

Many golfers have started here without much background in the sport. That should remain part of the club.

Current direction

More structure, not more barriers.

The club has historically been informal. The aim now is to keep the access while making expectations clearer for new and regular players.

That means better introductions, better guidance, respect for the course, pace of play, and basic etiquette explained plainly.

Facilities

Golf facilities only

The facilities relevant to this page are the ones that support playing and improving at golf.

Driving range

Covered bays for warm-ups, practice, and lessons.

Practice areas

Short-game and putting spaces for simple, regular work.

Coaching

Support for beginners and players working on specific parts of their game.

Buggy hire

Available when course conditions and daily status allow.

Clubhouse

A base for check-in, food, drink, and time after the round.

The best way to understand The Notleys is to play it: the valley holes, the river, the flatter back-nine stretches, and the parts of the course that reward repeat rounds.
